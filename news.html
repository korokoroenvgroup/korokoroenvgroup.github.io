NEWS & UPDATES

BNZ Closed For Good
 8th May, 2012

On Tuesday 8th of May, BNZ Closed for Good volunteers arrived at two sites in Korokoro.
A-team work clearing back rubbish and weeds at the base of Titiromoana Road (under the able supervision of Ruth Mansell).

B-Team started clearing, weeding and laying weed mats in the riparian planting on Mill Stream at the Cornish Street entrance. The planting was originally carried out as another BNZ Closed For Good project from 2 years ago.

At midday both teams united for a big push clearing back weeds and rubbish at the base of the Mill workers track and opening up a path to the larger car-park.

The project was a joint effort between KEG, the BNZ, LHCC and GWRC. (And Whittakers who provided the peanut slabs).

Thanks to Ken Marner for organising this.

ERMA Weed Clearing
 25 February
Approximately 50 ERMA staff and volunteers made a useful contribution by removing areas of Tradescantia in Galbraith's Gully down as far as the car park.

"Recycling in Korokoro is a go!"
6th July, 2011

"New recycling bins will be delivered to every Korokoro home later this month as part of a trial to further encourage recycling across the suburb.
Flexinets, which prevent recycled items from blowing away, will also be delivered.
This is a joint initiative between Hutt City Council and the Korokoro Environmental Group and if successful may be repeated in other areas."
